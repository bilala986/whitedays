White Days Info

The White Days are the 13th, 14th, and 15th of each Hijri month. These days are recommended for fasting based on the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ. Fasting during these days is considered virtuous and brings extra reward.

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said:

"The fasts of the three days of each month are like fasting the entire month."
عن أبي هريرة رضي الله عنه: قال رسول الله ﷺ: «صيام ثلاثة أيام من كل شهر، كصيام الدهر»

These days are called "Ayyam al-Beed" (White Days) because the moon is full and shines brightly.

The Quran encourages fasting in general:

"O you who have believed, decreed upon you is fasting as it was decreed upon those before you that you may become righteous."
يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا كُتِبَ عَلَيْكُمُ الصِّيَامُ كَمَا كُتِبَ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ (البقرة: 183)

Fasting on the White Days has additional benefits:

  • It helps purify the soul and body.
  • It is a Sunnah practice for increased reward.
  • It aligns with the natural lunar calendar and the rhythm of the moon.

Recommended practice:

  • Fast the 13th, 14th, and 15th of each lunar month.
  • Intend the fasts sincerely for Allah’s sake.
  • You may combine with voluntary prayers or dhikr.

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ also said:

"Fasting three days of every month is like fasting the whole year."
عن أبي هريرة رضي الله عنه قال: قال رسول الله ﷺ: «صيام ثلاثة أيام من كل شهر، كصيام الدهر»

How to Fast

Fasting (Ṣiyām) is a form of worship where a person abstains from food, drink, and other actions that invalidate the fast from dawn (Fajr) until sunset (Maghrib), intending the fast sincerely for Allah's sake. It is a highly recommended practice, especially during Ramadan, voluntary fasts, and the White Days.

The Qur’an commands fasting during Ramadan:

"O you who have believed, decreed upon you is fasting as it was decreed upon those before you that you may become righteous."
يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا كُتِبَ عَلَيْكُمُ الصِّيَامُ كَمَا كُتِبَ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ (البقرة: 183)

The Prophet ﷺ explained how to fast properly:

"Fasting is a shield; when one of you is fasting, he should avoid obscene language and foolish behavior. If someone fights him or insults him, let him say: ‘I am fasting.’"
عن أبي هريرة رضي الله عنه، قال: قال رسول الله ﷺ: «الصوم جنة، فإذا كان أحدكم صائمًا فلا يرفث ولا يصخب»

Other key rules for fasting include:

  • Intend your fast before dawn.
  • Abstain from food, drink, sexual activity, and other invalidating acts.
  • Break the fast immediately at sunset with a nutritious meal.
  • Maintain good manners, patience, and remembrance of Allah throughout the day.

Voluntary fasts and Sunnah practices:

  • Fasting on Mondays and Thursdays.
  • The White Days: 13th, 14th, and 15th of each lunar month.
  • Fasting the Day of Arafah (9th of Dhul-Hijjah) and Ashura (10th of Muharram).

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ emphasized the spiritual benefit of fasting:

"Whoever fasts Ramadan with faith and seeking reward, all his past sins will be forgiven."
عن ابن عباس رضي الله عنه: قال رسول الله ﷺ: «من صام رمضان إيمانًا واحتسابًا غفر له ما تقدم من ذنبه»

Virtues of White Days

The White Days (Ayyam al-Beed) are highly virtuous days for fasting, and the Prophet ﷺ emphasized their reward:

"Fasting three days of every month is like fasting the whole year."
عن أبي هريرة رضي الله عنه قال: قال رسول الله ﷺ: «صيام ثلاثة أيام من كل شهر، كصيام الدهر»

Fasting on these days is considered nafl (voluntary) but carries immense reward. It is a means of purification and spiritual elevation.

"The fasts of the three days of each month are like fasting the entire month."
عن أبي هريرة رضي الله عنه: قال رسول الله ﷺ: «صيام ثلاثة أيام من كل شهر، كصيام الدهر»

The White Days also align with the lunar cycle, particularly the full moon, highlighting the harmony between worship and nature.

"Fast on the days of white (13th, 14th, 15th of each month)."
الأيام البيض (الثالث عشر، الرابع عشر، والخامس عشر من كل شهر)

Additional virtues:

  • Forgiveness of minor sins.
  • Spiritual discipline and mindfulness of Allah.
  • Following the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ.

Fasting Recommendations

Fasting in Islam is a highly rewarding act of worship. Beyond the obligatory fasts of Ramadan, several voluntary (Nafl) fasts are recommended throughout the year. These include:

  • White Days (Ayyam al-Beed): 13th, 14th, 15th of each Hijri month.
  • Mondays and Thursdays: The Prophet ﷺ preferred fasting on these days.
  • Day of Arafah (9th Dhul-Hijjah): Highly recommended for non-pilgrims.
  • Day of Ashura (10th Muharram): Expiates sins of the previous year if done properly.
  • Six Days of Shawwal: Fasting six days after Eid al-Fitr complements Ramadan.

Prophet Muhammad ﷺ said:

"The best of fasting after Ramadan is the month of Allah, Muharram, and the best of prayer after the obligatory prayer is at night."
عن أبي هريرة رضي الله عنه قال: قال رسول الله ﷺ: «أفضل الصيام بعد رمضان شهر الله المحرم، وأفضل الصلاة بعد الفريضة صلاة الليل»

Fasting etiquette and rules:

  • Begin the fast at Fajr (dawn) and break it at Maghrib (sunset).
  • Intend the fast sincerely for Allah’s sake (niyyah).
  • Avoid anything that invalidates the fast, including food, drink, and inappropriate speech or actions.
  • It is recommended to start with Suhur (pre-dawn meal) and break the fast with Iftar (sunset meal), preferably dates and water.

Additional benefits of voluntary fasting:

  • Spiritual purification and increased consciousness of Allah.
  • Expiation of minor sins and cultivation of patience.
  • Following the Sunnah of the Prophet ﷺ, which brings immense reward.

Prophet ﷺ also said regarding Monday & Thursday fasting:

"Deeds are presented to Allah on Mondays and Thursdays, so I like my deeds to be presented while I am fasting."
عن أبي هريرة رضي الله عنه قال: قال رسول الله ﷺ: «تُعرض الأعمال يوم الاثنين والخميس، فأحب أن يُعرض عملي وأنا صائم»
